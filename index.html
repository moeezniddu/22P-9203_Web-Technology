Comprehensive Report

Islamabad (Special Correspondent)

Pakistan witnessed one of its darkest days in history when a military aircraft carrying President Muhammad Zia-ul-Haq crashed near Bahawalpur, claiming the lives of all 31 people aboard. The tragedy unfolded shortly after takeoff when the C-130 Hercules transport plane caught fire and plummeted to the ground. Among those who perished were several high-ranking officials including the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Committee, General Akhtar Abdur Rahman, and US Ambassador Arnold Raphel.

Eyewitnesses reported seeing flames engulf the aircraft moments after it departed from Bahawalpur, where the President had been observing military exercises. The plane lost control and crashed in a remote area, leaving no survivors. Security forces immediately cordoned off the crash site as rescue teams began the grim task of recovering bodies from the wreckage.

The nation plunged into mourning as news of the catastrophe spread across the country. Acting President Ghulam Ishaq Khan addressed the nation, calling for calm and announcing that a high-level investigation would be launched to determine the cause of the crash. Government officials have formed an inquiry committee to examine all possible factors that may have contributed to this devastating incident.

General Zia-ul-Haq had seized power in 1977 and led Pakistan for eleven years, during which he implemented Islamic reforms and played a crucial role in supporting Afghan resistance against Soviet forces. World leaders expressed shock and condolences, with US President Ronald Reagan describing the loss as significant for both Pakistan and the international community. The tragedy marks a pivotal moment in Pakistan's political history as the nation faces an uncertain future without its longtime leader.

Global Response

Washington: President Ronald Reagan expressed deep sorrow over the loss, stating that General Zia-ul-Haq was an outstanding leader who had been a true friend to the United States during challenging times.

Riyadh: King Fahd conveyed profound grief and emphasized that the Islamic world has lost a dedicated servant who worked tirelessly for Muslim unity and prosperity.

New Delhi: Indian Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi sent a message of condolence to the Pakistani government and people during this time of national tragedy.
